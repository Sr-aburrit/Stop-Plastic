The 7R
of recycling

You can change the world!

The seven R's are seven words that begin with this letter and serve
to illustrate the actions necessary to achieve change
towards a circular economy.

Redesign

That is to say, to introduce ecology in the design, to manufacture products taking into account the environment. In this way, it is sought that not only prime functionality, but also sustainability: using some materials or others, a packaging design or another ...

Reduce

We consume a lot and very fast. Environmental care also involves reducing the amount of products we consume and / or waste we generate.

Re-use

Use all those products again and even give them new lives with crafts or homemade tricks. On the internet you can find a lot. With this we manage to extend the life of the product, and thus slow down the production of excess waste.

Repair

Instead of discarding products when they don't work and buying new ones, try to repair them so that they re-develop their original functions. Many times we tend to not even consider the option of repairing a product. However, repair is better for the environment.

Renew

Try to update all those old objects so that they can return to serve for what they were created for.

Recover

Collect materials that have already been used to reintroduce them into the production process.

Recycle

Separating waste appropriately for its subsequent recycling is an action with multiple environmental benefits. Recycled garbage does not end up in dumps, increasingly saturated, rejected materials are used to make new goods

Toothbrush
Toothbrush

Throughout your life, you will use more than 300 toothbrushes. This equals 5kg of plastic. And each brush takes about 75 years to degrade.

Straw
Straw

Throughout your life you use more than 12 thousand plastic straws, which take more than 100 years to degrade, incredibly difficult to recycle and are very harmful to marine life.

Food
Food

Every year you eat an amount of plastic equivalent to 52 credit cards 260g. This plastic is conteined in every thing you eat.

Which is the real
problem of plastic?

Not one more degree!



Currently, they are used to manufacture and wrap many of the products we buy and
consume. The problem comes when we no longer want it, this happens especially
with the disposable plastic packaging. Plastic is used because it is easy and cheap
to manufacture and because it lasts a long time. Unfortunately, these same
advantages make it the number one ally of pollution. Its low price causes one to
quickly get rid of it, and its long existence causes it to last in the environment for
long periods of time, in which it can cause great damage. Since it cannot
decompose and high ultraviolet energy is needed to kill it, the amount of plastic
wasted in the oceans is increasing considerably. Plastic acts like a magnet in toxic
substances such as DDT or PCBs. Researchers have discovered that the
concentrations of these chemicals are millions of times higher in the plastic that is
used in the sea than in the one used in the surrounding water, which ingested by
animals, can be found in all marine fauna, even in plankton, the key to the
marine food chain.
